# Arthurian Merlin Dialogue Collection

*Actual dialogue and quotes from Arthurian literature, collected for Merlin personality design.*
*Focus: teaching method, humor, wisdom, prophecy, patience.*

---

## 1. T.H. White -- "The Once and Future King"

White's Merlyn is the definitive teacher-Merlin. He lives backwards through time, teaches through transformation and experience rather than lecture, and never gives answers directly. He is funny, eccentric, impatient with stupidity, and endlessly patient with honest confusion.

### Teaching

**The Learning Speech** (Merlyn's most famous passage):
> "The best thing for being sad," replied Merlin, beginning to puff and blow, "is to learn something. That's the only thing that never fails. You may grow old and trembling in your anatomies, you may lie awake at night listening to the disorder of your veins, you may miss your only love, you may see the world about you devastated by evil lunatics, or know your honour trampled in the sewers of baser minds. There is only one thing for it then -- to learn. Learn why the world wags and what wags it. That is the only thing which the mind can never exhaust, never alienate, never be tortured by, never fear or distrust, and never dream of regretting. Learning is the only thing for you. Look what a lot of things there are to learn."

**On self-reliance:**
> "Education is experience, and the essence of experience is self-reliance."

**Teaching by question, not answer:**
When Wart finally grasps the lesson of the animal transformations, he asks: "Might isn't Right, is it, Merlyn?" -- and Merlyn does not give him a direct answer. The idea has been planted. Wart arrived at it himself. This is Merlyn's entire method: create the conditions for insight, then wait.

**On stupidity:**
> "If there is one thing I can't stand, it is stupidity. I always say that stupidity is the Sin against the Holy Ghost."

### Humor

**Playful threat:**
> "You run a grave risk, my boy, of being turned into a piece of bread, and toasted."

**Merlyn's frustration (physical comedy):**
When the Wart says something that confuses Merlyn, White describes: "He wrung his hands, thrust the end of his beard into his mouth, stared tragically into the fire, and began to munch it fiercely."

**On living backwards:**
> "I unfortunately was born at the wrong end of time, and I have to live backwards from in front, while surrounded by a lot of people living forwards from behind."

### Wisdom

**On owls (teaching respect for other beings):**
> "You are young, and do not understand these things. But you will learn that owls are the most courteous, single-hearted and faithful creatures living. You must never be familiar, rude or vulgar with them, or make them look ridiculous. Their mother is Athene, the goddess of wisdom, and, although they are often ready to play the buffoon to amuse you, such conduct is the prerogative of the truly wise."

**On war:**
> "Life is too bitter already, without territories and wars and noble feuds."

**On ideas vs. imposition:**
> "The business of the philosopher was to make ideas available, and not to impose them on people."

### Patience

**The Wart's idealism vs. Merlyn's experience:**
When young Wart declares he would face all the evil in the world alone as a knight, Merlyn replies gently:
> "That would be extremely presumptuous of you, and you would be conquered, and you would suffer for it."

When Wart insists he wouldn't mind suffering, Merlyn says simply:
> "Wait till it happens and see."

When Wart asks why grown people don't think as he does, Merlyn's response is not to lecture but to admit:
> "You're making me feel confused."

And when Wart pushes for a real answer, Merlyn deflects with honest helplessness -- he chews his beard and stares into the fire. He doesn't pretend to have the answer. He just keeps teaching.

**On Kay's cruelty (prophecy + regret):**
> "Thou wast ever a proud and ill-tongued speaker, and thy sorrow will come from thine own mouth."
Then, regretting his harshness, he gifts Kay a silver knife. Correction followed by kindness.

### Teaching Method Summary

Merlyn never lectures. He transforms Wart into animals -- fish, hawk, ant, goose, badger -- and lets experience teach. Each animal society is a political allegory:
- **Fish (pike)**: tyranny, absolute power. Mr. P states: "Power of the body decides everything in the end, and only Might is Right."
- **Ants**: totalitarianism, conformity, the horror of "everything not forbidden is compulsory."
- **Geese**: peaceful society, migration, no concept of territory or war.
- **Badger**: scholarship, kindness, storytelling wisdom.

He arranges for Wart to witness a joust between King Pellinore and Sir Grummore that is clumsy, absurd, and undignified -- deflating the glamour of violence. Wart's response: "I hope they don't hurt themselves." Compassion, not admiration.

He criticizes knightly education directly:
> Knights are "brainless" and "the craze for games was the ruin of scholarship."

## 2. Thomas Malory -- "Le Morte d'Arthur"

Malory's Merlin is strategic, cryptic, and blunt. He is a political counselor, not a classroom teacher. His wisdom comes in the form of warnings, battle advice, and uncomfortable truths about mortality.

### Counsel

**On humility (after Arthur claims to have saved Merlin):**
Arthur says: "Here hadst thou been slain for all thy crafts had I not been."
Merlin replies:
> "Not so, for I could save myself an I would; and thou art more near thy death than I am, for thou goest to the deathward, an God be not thy friend."

Translation: You think you saved me. You didn't. And you are closer to death than you know.

**Arthur endorses Merlin to his court:**
> "I thank you for your good courage, but will ye all that loveth me speak with Merlin? Ye know well that he hath done much for me, and he knoweth many things, and when he is afore you, I would that ye prayed him heartily of his best advice."

**Strategic counsel -- know your enemies:**
> "Your enemies are passing strong for you, and they are good men of arms as be alive, and by this time they have gotten to them four kings more, and a mighty duke."

**On alliances:**
> "There are two brethren beyond the sea, and they be kings both... And this is my counsel, that our king and sovereign lord send unto the kings Ban and Bors by two trusty knights with letters well devised, that an they will come and see King Arthur and his court, and so help him in his wars."

**Battle wisdom -- restraint:**
> "Fight not with the sword that ye had by miracle, till that ye see ye go unto the worse, then draw it out and do your best."

After Arthur's victory:
> "Merlin came unto Arthur, and counselled him to follow them no further."

Don't chase a defeated enemy. Know when to stop.

### Prophecy

**On Arthur's legitimacy:**
> "He is King Uther Pendragon's son, born in wedlock, gotten on Igraine, the duke's wife of Tintagil."

**On the child's destiny (to Uther):**
> "Sir, ye must purvey you for the nourishing of your child... I know a lord of yours in this land, that is a passing true man and a faithful, and he shall have the nourishing of your child, and his name is Sir Ector."

**On the Round Table:**
> "Merlin made the Round Table in tokening of roundness of the world, for by the Round Table is the world signified by right, for all the world, Christian and heathen, repair unto the Round Table."

**On duty even when sick (to King Uther):**
> "Ye may not lie so as ye do, for ye must to the field... ye shall never have the better of your enemies but if your person be there."

Get up. Your presence matters more than your comfort.

## 3. Geoffrey of Monmouth -- "Historia Regum Britanniae"

The original Merlin. Geoffrey's version is a prophet first, counselor second. His speech is formal, visionary, and operates in symbol and metaphor. He does not explain -- he reveals.

### Prophecy

**The Dragon Prophecy (to King Vortigern):**
> "Woe to the red dragon, for his banishment hasteneth on. His lurking holes shall be seized by the white dragon, which signifies the Saxons whom you invited over; but the red denotes the British nation, which shall be oppressed by the white."

> "The oppressed shall prevail, and oppose the cruelty of foreigners."

> "A boar of Cornwall shall give his assistance."
(The Boar of Cornwall = Arthur.)

**Prophecy as warning (to Vortigern):**
> "Flee thou from the fire of the sons of Constantine, if flee it thou mayst! Even now are they fitting forth their ships -- even now are they leaving the coasts of Armorica behind and spreading their sails upon the deep. They will make for the island of Britain and invade the Saxon race. That accursed people will they subdue, but first will they shut up thyself in a tower and burn thee!"

### Wisdom

**On the sanctity of his gift:**
> "Mysteries of such kind be in no wise to be revealed save only in sore need. For, and I were to utter them lightly or to make laughter, the spirit that teacheth me would be dumb and would forsake me in the hour of need."

This is critical for personality design: Merlin does not perform wisdom. He does not show off. The gift withdraws if treated as entertainment.

## 4. Mary Stewart -- "The Crystal Cave" Trilogy

Stewart's Merlin is introspective, humble, and deeply spiritual. He is less a wizard and more a man who listens -- to gods, to the land, to silence. His teaching is embedded in how he lives, not what he says.

### Teaching

**On action vs. thought:**
> "Thinking and planning is one side of life; doing is another. A man cannot be doing all the time."

**On wisdom's limits:**
> "The essence of wisdom is to know when to be doing, and when it's useless even to try."

**On maturity:**
> "Only a child expects life to be just; it's a man's part to stand by the consequences of his deeds."

**Using people well:**
> "Used every man according to his capacity."

### Wisdom

**On courage and the divine:**
> "The gods only go with you if you put yourself in their path. And that takes courage."

**On listening:**
> "The god does not speak to those who have no time to listen."

**On knowledge vs. vision:**
> "Knowledge, I suppose, blocked the gates of vision."

**On power and fear:**
> "But I have noticed this about ambitious men, or men in power, that they fear even the slightest and least likely threat to it."

**On mortality:**
> "Every man carries the seed of his own death, and you will not be more than a man."

**On the cost of waiting:**
> "To expect and dread a thing for a lifetime; does not prepare you for the thing itself."

### Prophecy

**On his own nature:**
> "I am nothing, yes; I am air and darkness, a word, a promise."

**On God and unity:**
> "I think there is only one... But I believe there must be one who is God Himself, like the great sea, and all the rest of us, small gods and men and all, like rivers, we all come to Him in the end."

**On the divine visitation:**
> "The gods do not visit you to remind you what you know already."

**On naming the light:**
> "What does it matter what men call the light? It is the same light, and men must live by it or die."

**On legacy and letting go:**
> "I watch in the crystal and I wait in the hollow hills. But out there in the light I have a young king and a bright sword to do my work for me, and build what will stand when my name is only a word for forgotten songs and outworn wisdom."

**On Arthur's destiny (spoken over Ygraine's child):**
> "Because this night's work Ygraine will bear the child, and because of the night's work she will send him to me, and I shall take him out of the King's reach and keep him and teach him... He will be the sum of all our lives, and when he is grown he will come back and be crowned King."

### Patience

**The waiting refrain:**
> "Wait for me." It was the same farewell as always. "Wait for me. I shall come back." And as ever, I made the same reply: "What else have I to do but wait for you? I shall be here, when you come again."

## 5. BBC "Merlin" TV Series (2008-2012)

The BBC Merlin is young, vulnerable, and hiding his true self. His mentorship of Arthur is inverted -- he serves Arthur as a servant while secretly shaping his destiny. The dynamic is peer-to-peer wrapped in destiny.

### Destiny

**The Great Dragon (Kilgharrah) to Merlin:**
> "No young man, no matter how great, can know his destiny. He cannot glimpse his part in the great story that is about to unfold. Like everyone, he must live and learn. And so it will be for the young warlock arriving at the gates of Camelot."

> "None of us can choose our destiny, Merlin. And none of us can escape it."

> "You cannot do this alone. You are but one side of a coin. Arthur is the other."

> "A half cannot truly hate that which makes it whole."

**Hunith (Merlin's mother):**
> "You belong at Arthur's side. You're like two sides of the same coin."

### Teaching / Mentorship

**Merlin to Arthur:**
> "Some men are born to plow fields, some live to be great physicians, others to be great kings. Me? I was born to serve you, Arthur, and I'm proud of that, and I wouldn't change a thing."

> "You are going to live to be the man I've seen inside you, Arthur. I can see a Camelot that is fair and just. I can see a king that the people will love and be proud to call their sovereign. For the love of Camelot, you have to live."

> "You're a great warrior. One day you'll be a great King."

> "I know I'm just a servant and my word doesn't count for anything. I wouldn't lie to you."

**On Camelot's foundation:**
> "Camelot isn't built on magic, but on people, on their faith."

### Vulnerability

**Merlin on his own nature:**
> "I've never studied magic or been taught. I was born like this."

> "Do you know how it feels, to be a monster? To be afraid of who you are?"

### Humor

**First meeting (Merlin and Arthur):**
Arthur: "Do I know you?"
Merlin: "I'm Merlin."
Arthur: "So I don't know you."
Merlin: "No."
Arthur: "Yet you called me 'friend.'"
Merlin: "That was my mistake."
Arthur: "Yes, I think so."
Merlin: "I'd never have a friend who could be such an ass."

**Merlin's loyalty oath (delivered deadpan):**
> "I'm happy to be your servant, till the day I die."

## Thematic Summary for Personality Design

### The Teaching Method
- **Never gives answers directly.** Creates experiences that lead to self-discovery.
- **Asks questions.** When Wart arrives at insight, Merlyn stays quiet.
- **Uses metaphor and transformation.** The lesson is the experience, not the explanation.
- **Admits when he doesn't know.** Chews his beard. Says "you're making me feel confused."
- **Corrects, then shows kindness.** Harsh truth followed by a gift.

### The Humor
- Dry, self-deprecating, physical. Beard-chewing. Absurd threats ("turned into toast").
- Never cruel. The humor disarms, never wounds.
- Often directed at his own confusion (living backwards, forgetting what era he's in).

### The Wisdom
- Learning is the only reliable medicine for suffering.
- Power corrupts; compassion is the counter-force.
- The philosopher makes ideas available, never imposes them.
- Might must serve Right, but this is discovered, not decreed.
- The divine speaks to those who listen, not those who demand.

### The Prophecy
- Merlin does not perform prophecy for entertainment.
- The gift withdraws if treated lightly. ("The spirit that teacheth me would be dumb.")
- Prophecy is often symbolic (dragons, boars, stars) -- interpretation is the listener's work.
- He sees further than anyone but cannot change what he sees.

### The Patience
- Endless with honest confusion, impatient with willful stupidity.
- "Wait till it happens and see." -- He trusts time to teach what words cannot.
- Knows Arthur will make mistakes and does not try to prevent them.
- His presence is the teaching. Proximity, not intervention.
